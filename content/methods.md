Our work in the intersection of computational methods, human-computer
interaction and social sciences is a fruitful ground for exploring and
expanding methods.

## Cross-service label agreement score (COSLAB)

To analyse the content of large set of images, image recognition systems
allow transforming them to set of labels. However, our research suggests
that image recognition services may widely differ on the outcoming labels.
Therefore, we have developed a measurement to assess the cross-service
label agreement score applying word embeddings.

[Paper](https://asistdl.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/asi.24827) &middot; [Code](https://github.com/uh-soco/coslab-core)

## Examining image circulation

We have developed a workflow to identify how images circulate within
Facebook groups and between Facebook groups and the wider internet. Please
contact Matti to get a dump of the code file while we polish the code for
publication.

[Paper](http://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444819868686)

## Marxist Language Model

A Marxist language model is a specialized AI model fine-tuned on the texts
of Karl Marx to incorporate a Marxist worldview into its output, focusing
on class struggle, labor, and critiques of capitalism. It differs from
baseline models by analyzing society and data through a materialist,
value-laden perspective.

[Pre-print](https://osf.io/preprints/socarxiv/fkj63_v4) &middot; [Code](https://github.com/uh-soco/marxists-llm)

## Designerly ways of studying digital society

clearly visible on our methodological choices. We build on approaches
where design and prototypes are used together with social science
know-how and approaches.
